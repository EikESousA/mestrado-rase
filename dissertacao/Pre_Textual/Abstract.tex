\begin{resumo}[Abstract]
 \begin{otherlanguage*}{english}
    The adoption of Building Information Modeling (BIM) is digitally transforming the construction industry by centralizing information throughout a project's entire lifecycle, increasing accuracy, reducing errors, and optimizing resources. However, the growing use of BIM also presents challenges, such as managing large volumes of data and integrating complex regulatory standards. As BIM-based projects become more common, there is a significant increase in data generation, requiring advanced technological solutions.
    The application of Artificial Intelligence (AI), particularly through Machine Learning (ML), has significantly contributed to automating processes in the industry. For instance, AI models have been used for automatic object detection in images or even improving assembly processes in construction through design rules. However, in the context of regulatory standards in Architecture, Engineering, and Construction (AEC), gaps still exist in the integration of these technologies at national and international levels due to the semantic complexity and lack of standardization of these documents.    
    A promising solution is the RASE methodology, which converts regulatory standards into computable data, enabling automatic calculations and validations in real projects. Large Language Models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT and Llama 3, have been explored to interpret regulatory documents, facilitating semantic analysis and the extraction of relevant information. However, these approaches face limitations, such as difficulties in interpreting documents with highly technical language and the need for specific training with AEC sector data, which can restrict their broader applicability.    
    This research proposes developing three LLMs based on LLaMa 3 to understand and transform standards into structured rules tailored for the AEC sector. The three specific models will be developed and trained using distinct approaches to convert standards into RASE patterns, resulting in verifiable data within Revit software. Techniques such as Prompt Engineering, Fine-Tuning, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) will be employed to enhance the models' performance. Validation will involve applying the LLMs to real projects in Revit, identifying regulatory compliance through color-coded visualizations.
   \vspace{\onelineskip}
 
   \noindent 
   \textbf{Keywords}: Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, LLM, LLaMa, BIM, RASE.
 \end{otherlanguage*}
\end{resumo}
